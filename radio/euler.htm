Euler's Constant

Euler's Constant (or Mascheroni's constant) is the limit (as n goes to infinity) of

1 + 1/2 + 1/3 + 1/4 + 1/5 + ... + 1/n - log n.

The Euler-Mascheroni constant is denoted by the greek letter gamma (γ) (or sometimes C) and has the numerical value approximately 0.57721566

Even though over 1,000,000 digits of this number have been calculated (http://dipmat.unife.it/Root/d-Mathematics/d-Number-theory/t-Eulers-constant-Haible), it is not yet known if it is a rational number (the ratio of two integers a/b). But if it is rational, the denominator (b) must have more than 244,663 digits!
